Our Mission

We dream of a world where butterflies flourish within their native ecosystems, enriching biodiversity and ensuring the vitality of our natural landscapes across the United States. Our vision is for a future where the activities of humans seamlessly integrate with the natural world, nurturing the existence of these essential pollinators. Our mission is to enlighten and advocate for butterfly conservation, offering in-depth resources and captivating materials that underscore the critical ecological roles butterflies play, from pollination to signaling the health of our environment. Our goal is to motivate both individuals and communities to engage in significant efforts that bolster the populations of these indispensable insects.

Why Butterflies Matter

Butterflies are facing new challenges, with many species experiencing significant declines due to habitat loss, climate change, and other human-induced factors. Species like the Monarch and the Miami Blue are symbolic of the broader threats to biodiversity and the urgent need for organized and concerted conservation efforts. Our mission matters because it represents a proactive step toward reversing these declines, ensuring that butterflies continue to play their critical roles in pollination and serving as key indicators of environmental health. By fostering a community committed to conservation, education, and advocacy, we can create a collective impact that protects butterflies and, by extension, the ecosystems they inhabit.

Are Butterflies at Risk?

Some butterfly species are indeed considered endangered or at risk, facing threats from habitat loss, climate change, pollution, and the use of pesticides. The exact status can vary widely among species and regions. In the United States, for example, the Monarch butterfly is one of the most well-known cases of a butterfly species facing significant threats. While not all species are endangered, many are experiencing declines that could lead to their being threatened in the future. Specifically, the Monarch butterfly has seen a dramatic decrease in population over the past few decades, attributed to the loss of milkweed plants (which serve as the sole food source for Monarch caterpillars) due to agricultural practices and urbanization, as well as changes in climate patterns affecting their migration routes. Another example is the Miami Blue butterfly, once widespread across coastal areas in southern Florida, which is now considered critically endangered due to habitat destruction and fragmentation. The status of butterfly species can be monitored through the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List, national lists, and research conducted by conservation organizations, providing updated information on which species are at risk and the level of their endangerment.

Conservation Efforts

Our conservation efforts focus on several key areas:

  • Habitat Restoration: We support projects aimed at restoring and maintaining habitats that are crucial for butterfly survival, such as milkweed planting for monarch butterflies.
  • We believe knowledge is power. Our website provides resources and information on how individuals and communities can contribute to butterfly conservation.
  • At-Home Science: Get involved with science projects at home or in school that track butterfly populations and migrations. Your observations can help scientists gather vital data.
  • Supporting Legislation: Advocating for policies and legislation that protect butterflies and their habitats is crucial. Its important to stay up to date an informed on how to support any and all conservation efforts.
